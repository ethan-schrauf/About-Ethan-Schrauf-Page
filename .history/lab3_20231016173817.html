Colorado Columbine

arrow_forward

The Colorado Columbine (Aquilegia coerulea) is the official state flower of Colorado, known for its striking blue-violet petals and white center. This wildflower can be found in mountainous regions and symbolizes the rugged beauty of the Rocky Mountains. It's a delicate and enchanting bloom, often attracting pollinators with its unique appearance.

Indian Paintbrush

arrow_forward

The Indian Paintbrush (Castilleja) is a vibrant and diverse wildflower species with a broad range of colors, but it's most commonly associated with its striking red, orange, or scarlet hues. Its vibrant appearance, often found in mountainous and alpine regions, makes it a captivating sight in the wild. Indian Paintbrush has a unique relationship with certain fungi, known as mycorrhizal, which helps it thrive in various ecosystems.

Larkspur

arrow_forward

Larkspur (Delphinium) is a beautiful wildflower that is part of the buttercup family and is known for its spiky, vertical clusters of bright and delicate, spurred flowers. Larkspur comes in various colors, including shades of blue, purple, and pink, and is a common sight in meadows and open fields. These elegant flowers are often sought after for their ornamental beauty and their symbolism in the language of flowers, representing an open heart and ardent attachment.
